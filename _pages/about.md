I am a Ph.D. student in Business and Public Policy at the Haas School of Business, University of California, Berkeley.

My research lies at the intersection of development economics, political economy, and organizational economics. I study the political economy of development, with an emphasis on the organizational foundations of informal institutions: the local arrangements through which communities solve collective-action problems where formal state capacity is limited. My current work examines these questions in the Democratic Republic of Congo by studying how cooperation is organized across ethnic divisions, how communities interpret and respond to shocks, and how formal legal institutions reshape existing systems of local authority.

Before Berkeley, I earned an M.Sc. in Economics from PUC-Rio and a B.A. in Economics from IBMEC.

You can reach me at [gabrielgranato@berkeley.edu](mailto:gabrielgranato@berkeley.edu).
